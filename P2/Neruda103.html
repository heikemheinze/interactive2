YOUR FEET



When I cannot look at your face

    I look at your feet.


Your feet

    of arched bone,

your hard little feet.


I know that they support you,

    and that your sweet weight

rises upon them.


Your waist and your breasts,

    the doubled purple

of your nipples,

    the sockets of your eyes

that have just flown away,

    your wide fruit mouth,

your red tresses,

    my little tower.




But I love your feet

     only because

    they walked

     upon the earth

    and upon

     the wind

     and upon the waters,

     until they found me.

Pablo Neruda
