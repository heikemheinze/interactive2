SCALE:

How big is a nanometer: Nano means "billionth", so a nanometer is one billionth of a meter. In other words, the nanoscale is 1000 times smaller than the microscopic scale and a billion (1000 million) times smaller than the world of meters that we live in. Due to dominance of spatial, temporal and dynamic variations in the nanoscale era, it is prudent for companies to try to mitigate the potential risks of nanoparticles during the design stage rather than downstream during manufacturing or customer use. Ways to solve these challenges include having precise design margins and adaptive design techniques..
