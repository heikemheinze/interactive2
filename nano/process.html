Why Nanotechnology?

Having looked through scientific journals, news articles, and discussed nanotechnology and design within my Current Issues in Design class, I came to find that I was more interested in the content. I wanted to try something I have done only once before: to try and find commonalities in science and graphic design in order to bring it into my portfolio. Outside of my own personal work, I also felt it was an important topic to discuss because nanotechnology is still a topic that isn't familiar to the general public (far less so in the visual spectrum of things) or discussed and what is worse is that little work has been made in terms of preparing for a future that, frankly, has already been here for a while...and nanotechnology is a large part of that.

As a term, I will admit that "nanotechnology" can seem rather vague... It contains little information as to form because it is more, perhaps, a concept that initially was rooted from a paper by Richard Feyman titled There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom.
